Understanding Skin Cancer Types

Early detection is crucial for successful treatment. Learn about different types of skin cancer and their characteristics:

Melanoma

The most dangerous form of skin cancer. Melanomas often resemble moles and can develop from them. They can be found anywhere on the body.

Key Characteristics:
  • Asymmetrical shape
  • Irregular borders
  • Variable colors
  • Diameter > 6mm
  • Evolution over time

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common type of skin cancer. Usually appears as a flesh-colored, pearl-like bump or a pinkish patch of skin.

Common Signs:
  • Waxy, scar-like appearance
  • Raised, pearly borders
  • May bleed easily
  • Often found on sun-exposed areas

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

The second most common type of skin cancer. Appears as red, scaly patches, open sores, or warts.

Warning Signs:
  • Rough, scaly patches
  • Raised edges
  • May be tender to touch
  • Can grow rapidly

Prevention Tips

  • Use broad-spectrum sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Avoid peak sun hours (10 AM - 4 PM)
  • Wear protective clothing
  • Regular skin self-examinations
  • Annual professional skin checks
